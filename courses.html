Kindergarten

from 3 years old

age psychology

The critical period of language absorption and acquisition.

Quick to remember and forget, lots of energy but a short attention span.

Play and learn, love hands on activities.

teaching objectives

To develop natural language reflexes and expressions in very short and simple conversations.

To form very basic creative and critical thinking skills, and confidently demonstrate communication and cooperation skills in daily activities.

To recognize sounds and read short words. The child can start to independently write letters.

Primary English

From 6 years old

age psychology

At this stage, children are starting to use logical thinking to understand the world around them.

They love exciting and competitive activities.

They are actively developing four language skills: listening, speaking, reading, and writing.

teaching objectives

To be able to partake in a basic conversation about a variety of topics.

To form and develop 21st-century skills: communication, collaboration, creativity, critical thinking.

To develop a basic understanding of grammar, as well as a range of vocabulary and a basic proficiency in all 4 skills: reading, writing, speaking, and listening; start to develop basic academic skills - how to prepare for and approach exams.

Secondary English

from 11 years old

age psychology

Students begin to develop their own sense of self within the greater world around them, they have their own views and opinions, and tend to be guided by their own preferences and judgements.

They are interested in deep thinking activities, independent research, and expressing personal opinions.

Children begin to have directions for their future interests and careers, and tend to make friends around the world. However, important exams can make your child anxious and lose confidence.

teaching objectives

To develop greater fluency and coherence in English with the ability to express themselves well and awareness of more complex pronunciation features.

Develop a forward-thinking mindset, proficiently applying 21st-century skills: communication, collaboration, creativity, critical thinking.
